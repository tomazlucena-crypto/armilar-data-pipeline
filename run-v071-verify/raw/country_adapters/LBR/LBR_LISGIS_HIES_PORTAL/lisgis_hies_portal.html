Thematic Report on Agriculture in Liberia

The Agricultural economy is mostly subsistence and less monetized. We observed that one third (30.2 %) of the total households in Liberia are agricultural households, most of which are rural residents. There are slightly more males than females in these households, and about 0.5 % of the agricultural population lives with at least one form of disability.

Liberia Agriculture Census, 2022/2023

The Liberia Institute of Statistics and Geo-Information Services (LISGIS), in collaboration with the Ministry of Agriculture with funding from the World Bank and technical Support from the United Nations Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) and International Fund for Agriculture Development (IFAD), will be conducting an Agriculture Census in all the 15 Counties of Liberia between January and March 2024.


The Liberia Agriculture Census is the first to be conducted in Liberia and will use digital technology. The census will cover all Agriculture-related activities such as crop production, livestock rearing, forestry, poultry, and Aqua Culture. It will target all Agricultural establishments, including households, smallholder farmers, commercial and private farmers, and Agro-Industrial Companies.

Industrial Statistics - June 2025

The year-on-year inflation rate for Liberia for June 2025 was 9.9%, 1.8 percentage point lower than the previous month. The month’s YoY inflation rate is the percentage change in the Consumer Price Index (CPI) of June 2025 and June 2024. The inflation rate increased by 3.7 percentage points from June 2024. The month-on-month inflation in June 2025 was 0.4%. The MoM inflation rate in May 2025 was 0.2%.

Social Statistics

The office of the Department of Social Statistics is clothed with the responsibility of conducting studies on socio- economic analysis of data collected from surveys and censuses undertaken by the Department of Statistics and Data Processing.
